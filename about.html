Amortization Calculations

Understand how to calculate the annual cost of your project funded by a loan.

Definition of Amortization

Amortization is the gradual repayment of a loan through scheduled payments or the spreading of a long-term asset's cost over time in accounting. Loan payments combine interest and principal, while businesses amortize expenses like patents or machinery over their useful lives.

Formula

To calculate the annual payment for a loan:

  • P: Principal amount (total project cost)
  • i: Monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12)
  • y: Total number of payments (loan term in years × 12)
Annual Payment Formula:
Image courtesy of Google
A = P × [i(1 + i)y] / [(1 + i)y − 1]

Steps to Calculate

  1. Determine the loan principal (P).
  2. Calculate the monthly interest rate (i).
  3. Find the total number of payments (y).
  4. Plug the values into the formula above.
  5. Compute the result to determine your annual cost.

Example

We will now calculate the monthly payment for a project cost of $50,000, with a 5% annual interest rate over a 10-year loan period:

Variable Value
Principal (P) $50,000
Annual Interest Rate 5% (0.05)
Monthly Interest Rate (i) 0.004167
Loan Term (y) 10 years (120 months)
Monthly Payment (A) $530.50

Amortization Formula: A = P × [i(1 + i)y] / [(1 + i)y − 1]

Steps

  1. Monthly Interest Rate (i): 0.05 / 12 = 0.004167
  2. Apply the Formula: A = 50,000 × [0.004167 × (1.004167)120] / [(1.004167)120 − 1]
  3. Calculate: A ≈ $530.50
